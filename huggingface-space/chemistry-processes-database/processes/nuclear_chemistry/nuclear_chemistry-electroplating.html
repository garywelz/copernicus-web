Nuclear Fusion (D–T)

Chemistry Nuclear Chemistry Complexity: high
← Back to Chemistry Database Programming Framework

Description

Fusion joins light nuclei into a heavier one: only when enough energy is supplied to tunnel through the Coulomb barrier do deuterium and tritium fuse, releasing energy from the mass defect, and net power requires meeting the Lawson confinement criterion.
