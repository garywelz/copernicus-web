Fischer Esterification

Chemistry Organic Chemistry Complexity: medium
← Back to Chemistry Database Programming Framework

Description

Acid-catalyzed Fischer esterification condenses a carboxylic acid and an alcohol: protonation activates the carbonyl, the alcohol adds to form a tetrahedral intermediate, and loss of water gives the ester; the reaction is an equilibrium driven by removing water or using excess reagent.
