## Budget Justification Summary

This budget supports a 3-year research project to develop and evaluate an AI-enabled assisted research platform. The budget allocates:

- **35% to Senior Personnel:** PI effort (6 months/year) for research leadership and technical development
- **15% to Other Personnel:** Research assistant/developer support plus student user testers for alpha/beta testing and collaborative validation
- **10% to Fringe Benefits:** CUNY GC rates (PI summer 25.05%; part-time personnel 8% + 0.895% MTA)
- **22% to Other Direct Costs:** Cloud infrastructure, consultants, materials ($15,000), publication, other
- **34% to Indirect Costs:** CUNY Graduate Center research rate (54% of MTDC)

**Key Cost Drivers:**
1. **Personnel (67% combined):** Research-intensive work requires substantial PI and support personnel effort, plus student user testing for usability evaluation and collaborative validation
2. **Cloud Infrastructure (17% of total):** Production GCP services and multi-model LLM API costs are essential for the research
3. **Travel (4%):** Necessary for NSF meetings, conferences, and user study site visits

**Student User Testing Component:**
The student user testing allocation ($80,160 over 3 years) supports a critical research component: collaborative validation and real-world usability testing. Students will use the platform to create research briefings, test workflows, and participate in collective verification of AI outputs. This approach grounds the research in actual use cases while establishing mechanisms for collaborative intelligence that improve system reliability.

**Year-by-Year Variations:**
- **Year 1:** Higher equipment costs for initial setup
- **Year 2:** Increased personnel and cloud costs as user studies begin
- **Year 3:** Focus on evaluation and documentation, slightly lower cloud costs

This budget is consistent with typical NSF CISE Core awards and supports the research objectives outlined in the proposal. The allocation reflects the technical nature of the work, the need for production-scale infrastructure, and the importance of evaluation and dissemination activities.
