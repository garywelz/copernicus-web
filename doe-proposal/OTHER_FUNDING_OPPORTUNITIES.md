# Other US Federal and NY State Funding — Sooner Than DOE FY 2027?

**Goal:** Identify opportunities with deadlines or opening dates **before** DOE FY 2027 Phase I Release 1 (LOI ~late Aug 2026, full application ~Oct 2026).

---

## Federal SBIR/STTR — Reauthorization Limbo

Federal SBIR/STTR authority **expired September 30, 2025**. As of early 2026, reauthorization was still pending (e.g. S.1573 / H.R.3169). Until Congress reauthorizes:

- **NSF SBIR:** Project Pitch and new submissions are **paused**. When reopened, NSF has multiple Phase I windows per year (no single annual deadline) and requires an approved Project Pitch before a full proposal. Could offer a deadline **sooner than** DOE FY 2027 if reauthorization happens in early 2026.
- **NIH SBIR:** Not accepting new applications until reauthorization. When active, standard dates include April 5, September 5, January 5/8. Strong **mission fit** to an NIH Institute/Center (health, biomedical) is required — CopernicusAI would need a clear health-research angle.
- **DOD SBIR:** Typically three cycles per year. Also paused pending reauthorization. When active, topics are very specific (solicitation topics); would need a topic that fits research tools / data / AI for defense or dual-use.

**Action:** Monitor reauthorization (e.g. [sbir.gov](https://www.sbir.gov), [seedfund.nsf.gov](https://seedfund.nsf.gov), agency sites). When it passes, check **NSF** (Project Pitch → full proposal) and **NIH** (if you can frame the work for a specific IC) for the next open deadlines — they could be **before** DOE FY 2027.

## NIST SBIR (Department of Commerce)

- **FY 2026** Phase I solicitation was “to be announced” as of recent updates. NIST explicitly includes **Artificial Intelligence** as one of several research areas.
- **Award:** Phase I up to ~$100,000, ~6 months; no cost share required. Proposals must align with NIST technical areas or use of NIST-patented technology.
- **Fit:** AI, data, software tools for research or standards could align if the solicitation includes a topic that matches knowledge systems / metadata / research tools.

**Action:** Sign up for the [NIST SBIR Newsletter](https://www.nist.gov/tpo/small-business-innovation-research-program-sbir) and watch [nist.gov/tpo](https://www.nist.gov/tpo) for the FY 2026 NOFO and deadline. If the release is in spring/summer 2026, the Phase I deadline could be **before** DOE FY 2027.

## Other DOE (ARPA-E)

- **ARPA-E** runs separate funding opportunities (not the same as Office of Science SBIR). Solicitations are topic-specific and open/close on their own schedule.
- Focus is energy/climate/cleantech. CopernicusAI would need a clear energy or climate research-tools angle to fit.

**Action:** Periodically check [ARPA-E eXCHANGE](https://arpa-e-foa.energy.gov) for open FOAs. Only pursue if a topic clearly fits “research tools / data / AI for energy or climate.”

## New York State

### NYSTAR Innovation Matching Grants (IMG)

- **Purpose:** **Match** funding for companies that have **won** (or are applying for) a federal SBIR/STTR award. Not a substitute for federal SBIR.
- **Amounts:** Up to $100K (Phase I) or $200K (Phase II); max 50% of federal award. Use: commercialization (business planning, IP, marketing, legal), not duplicate of federal scope.
- **Eligibility:** NY small business, 100 or fewer employees; federal SBIR/STTR within 12 months.
- **Status:** Program has had high demand; next round TBA. To be notified when applications reopen: email **IMGProgram@esd.ny.gov** with subject **NOTIFY**.

**Action:** Once you **win** DOE FY 2027 (or any federal SBIR), apply for NYSTAR IMG match. No need to wait — you can email NOTIFY now so you get the next round announcement.

### Other NY State (ESD, NYSERDA)

- **Empire State Development (ESD):** Besides NYSTAR, ESD runs other innovation and small-business programs. Worth checking [esd.ny.gov](https://esd.ny.gov) for grants that do **not** require a federal SBIR first.
- **NYSERDA:** Energy/cleantech focus. Only relevant if you have an energy/climate angle; otherwise lower priority.

## Summary: What to Do Sooner

| Opportunity              | Possible timing vs DOE FY 2027 | Action |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------|--------|
| **NSF SBIR**             | Sooner if reauth. in 2026      | Watch for reauthorization; when open, submit Project Pitch, then full proposal to a relevant window. |
| **NIH SBIR**             | Sooner if reauth. (e.g. Apr 5)  | Only if you can frame for an NIH IC; watch reauthorization and next due dates. |
| **NIST SBIR**            | Possibly sooner (FY26 TBA)      | Subscribe to NIST SBIR newsletter; when FY 2026 NOFO posts, check AI topic and deadline. |
| **DOE FY 2027 Release 1**| Oct 2026 full app              | Submit LOI in PAMS when FOA posts (~late Aug 2026); then full proposal. |
| **NYSTAR IMG**           | After you win federal SBIR     | Email NOTIFY to IMGProgram@esd.ny.gov; apply once you have a federal award. |

**Bottom line:** The only federal SBIR cycle you can **definitely** plan for today is **DOE FY 2027 Release 1**. **NIST** (AI topic) and **NSF** (once reopened) are the best candidates for something **sooner**; both depend on solicitation release and, for NSF/NIH/DOD, on reauthorization. NY State’s soonest *direct* benefit is **NYSTAR match** after a federal win — get on the NOTIFY list now.
