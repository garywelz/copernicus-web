# DOE Research Security Training - Guide and Resources
## Required for DOE SBIR Proposals (Effective May 1, 2025)

---

## Requirement Overview

**Effective Date:** May 1, 2025

**Requirement:** All covered individuals must complete research security training **prior to** DOE proposal submission.

**Covered Individuals:**
- Principal Investigators
- Key Personnel
- Anyone involved in the research project

**Compliance:** Training must be completed and documented before proposal submission.

---

## Where to Get Research Security Training

### Option 1: SECURE Center Consolidated Training Module (CTM) — Recommended for DOE

**Explicitly recognized by DOE** (no additional certification needed). NSF, NIH, DOE, and DOD all recognize completion as compliant.

- **URL:** https://www.secure-center.org/ctm
- **Format:** Online condensed training module (Viewable Version with Certificate)
- **Duration:** Approximately 1 hour
- **Cost:** Free
- **Certificate:** Downloadable upon completion from the SECURE Center site

**Steps:**
1. Go to https://www.secure-center.org/ctm
2. Use the **Viewable Version with Certificate** link (e.g. securecenter.uw.edu/CTM-1-2/story.html)
3. Complete the module
4. Download/save the completion certificate (PDF)
5. Include the **SECURE Center CTM certificate** in the DOE proposal submission

**Note:** Other training (e.g. "Introduction to Research Security") may be useful as background; for DOE compliance, use the SECURE Center CTM certificate.

### Option 2: Other Training (Applicant Certifies)

**DOE policy:** Training that is not the SECURE Center CTM may be used if the Prime Applicant certifies that it meets the requirements under 42 USC 19234 (cybersecurity, international collaboration/travel, foreign interference, proper use of funds, disclosure, conflict of commitment, conflict of interest). No pre-approval by DOE is required.

- **DOE requirement page:** https://www.energy.gov/ia/research-security-training-requirement
- **FAL 2025-02:** https://www.energy.gov/management/pf-2025-04-fal-2025-02-research-security-training-requirements-all-rd-financial

## Training Topics Covered

Typical research security training covers:
- **Foreign Influence:** Identifying and managing foreign influence risks
- **Export Controls:** Understanding export control regulations
- **Intellectual Property:** Protecting IP and managing collaborations
- **Data Security:** Securing research data and information
- **Conflict of Interest:** Identifying and managing conflicts
- **Cybersecurity:** Protecting against cyber threats

---

## Documentation Requirements

### What You Need to Provide

1. **Training Completion Certificate**
   - Official certificate from training provider
   - Must show completion date
   - Must be dated before proposal submission date

2. **Training Record**
   - Document which training was completed
   - Date of completion
   - Training provider

3. **In Proposal Submission**
   - Include training completion information in proposal
   - May need to upload certificate as attachment
   - Reference in compliance section

---

## Steps to Complete Training

### Step 1: Identify Training Provider
- Visit DOE Research Security Training portal
- Or check with DOE program officer for approved providers

### Step 2: Register and Complete Training
- Create account if needed
- Complete all required modules
- Pass any required assessments

### Step 3: Obtain Certificate
- Download completion certificate
- Save PDF copy for records
- Note completion date

### Step 4: Include in Proposal
- Reference training completion in proposal
- Upload certificate if required
- Include in compliance checklist

---

## Timeline Recommendation

**Recommended Timeline:**
- **4-6 weeks before submission:** Complete training
- **2-3 weeks before submission:** Verify certificate received
- **1 week before submission:** Include in final proposal package

**Do NOT wait until last minute** - training must be completed BEFORE submission.

## Important Notes

1. **Training Must Be Completed BEFORE Submission**
   - Cannot submit proposal and complete training later
   - Proposal will be rejected if training not completed

2. **Keep Records**
   - Save certificate PDF
   - Note completion date
   - May need for future proposals or audits

3. **Check Specific FOA Requirements**
   - Each Funding Opportunity Announcement may have specific requirements
   - Verify exact training requirements in current DOE SBIR FOA

4. **Key Personnel**
   - If you have key personnel or subcontractors, they may also need training
   - Verify requirements for all project participants
