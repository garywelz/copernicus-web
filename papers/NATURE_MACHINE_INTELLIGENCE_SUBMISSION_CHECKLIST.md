Copy the abstract from the paper (lines 11-15):
```
This vision paper proposes "Knowledge Engines" as a framework for understanding how intelligent systems—both human and artificial—systematically discover, integrate, and generate knowledge. We argue that history's greatest minds functioned as knowledge engines, processing information through cycles of ingestion, analysis, synthesis, and communication, guided by curiosity, fearlessness, and willingness to challenge established beliefs.

We propose a taxonomy of nine integrated capabilities—ingestion, digestion, analysis, calculation, comparison, connection, association, analogy, and multi-modal communication—synthesizing decades of prior work in expert systems, knowledge representation, and cognitive architectures for the LLM era. Our framework emphasizes that ambitious goals like "finding a cure for cancer" require comprehensive integrated systems, not merely powerful models, combining AI capabilities with structured processes, specialized tools, and systematic approaches.

We present CopernicusAI as a working implementation of the Knowledge Engine framework, demonstrating feasibility through a fully deployed system with 12,000+ indexed research papers, operational knowledge graph visualization, vector search, and RAG capabilities. While extensive validation remains necessary, the system demonstrates that the framework can be instantiated in practice. This vision paper aims to establish "Knowledge Engine" as a generic term describing systems that systematically transform information into knowledge, providing both a theoretical blueprint and a concrete implementation for future research.
```

Dear Editors,

I am submitting a Perspective article entitled "A Vision for AI-Powered Knowledge Engines: A Framework for Systematic Knowledge Discovery and Integration" for consideration for publication in Nature Machine Intelligence.

Why this paper fits Nature Machine Intelligence:

This paper proposes a novel framework for understanding and building AI-powered knowledge systems, directly aligned with the journal's focus on intelligent systems and AI research. The framework synthesizes decades of AI research into a coherent taxonomy of nine integrated capabilities, providing both theoretical foundations and a concrete implementation (CopernicusAI). The work addresses a critical gap in current AI discourse: while individual AI capabilities are well-established, there is limited systematic guidance on how to integrate them into comprehensive knowledge systems that can achieve ambitious research goals.

Novel Contribution:

While individual components (knowledge graphs, RAG, vector search) are well-established, our systematic integration framework—organized around a nine-capability taxonomy—provides a coherent approach for designing knowledge systems. We present CopernicusAI as a working implementation demonstrating feasibility, with 12,000+ indexed papers and operational knowledge graph, vector search, and RAG capabilities. The framework emphasizes that achieving ambitious goals like "finding a cure for cancer" requires comprehensive integrated systems, not merely powerful models—a perspective that has significant implications for AI system design and deployment.

Article Type:

This is submitted as a Perspective article, as it presents a forward-looking framework and vision for knowledge systems, rather than reporting new experimental results. The paper synthesizes prior work, proposes a novel taxonomy, and demonstrates feasibility through a concrete implementation, making it well-suited for the Perspective format.

Preprint Status:
